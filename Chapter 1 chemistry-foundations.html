The Scientific Method

The scientific method is a systematic approach to gaining knowledge through observation and experimentation.

1. Ask a Question

Begin with curiosity about a natural phenomenon or process.

Example: "Does temperature affect the rate of a chemical reaction?"

2. Research

Gather information about what is already known on the topic.

3. Form a Hypothesis

Make an educated guess that answers your question and can be tested.

Example: "If the temperature is increased, then the rate of reaction will increase."

4. Experiment

Design and conduct experiments to test your hypothesis.

Control variables: Keep all factors constant except for the one being tested.

5. Analyze Data

Organize and analyze the results of your experiment.

6. Draw Conclusions

Determine if your data supports or refutes your hypothesis.

7. Communicate Results

Share your findings with the scientific community for peer review.

Scientific Notation

Scientists use scientific notation to express very large or very small numbers:

Format: a × 10b where 1 ≤ a < 10 and b is an integer

Example: 0.00000456 = 4.56 × 10-6

Significant Figures

The number of digits that carry meaningful information about a measurement.

Rules:

  • Non-zero digits are always significant
  • Zeros between significant digits are significant
  • Leading zeros are never significant
  • Trailing zeros after a decimal point are significant

Laboratory Safety & Techniques

Essential Safety Rules

👁️

Always Wear Safety Goggles

Protect your eyes from chemicals and flying debris at all times in the lab.

🥼

Wear Appropriate Clothing

Lab coats, closed-toe shoes, and tie back long hair.

🔬

No Unauthorized Experiments

Never perform unauthorized experiments or modify procedures without permission.

🚿

Know Safety Equipment Location

Locate eyewash stations, safety showers, fire extinguishers, and exits before beginning work.

⚗️

Handle Chemicals Properly

Never taste or directly smell chemicals. Use wafting technique for odors.

Common Lab Techniques

⚖️

Measuring Mass

Use analytical balances for precise measurements. Always tare the balance with a container before adding substances.

🧪

Measuring Volume

Use graduated cylinders, pipettes, or burettes depending on the precision required.

🔥

Heating Substances

Use water baths for gentle heating. For direct heating, use Bunsen burners with caution.

Isotopes

Isotopes are atoms of the same element with different numbers of neutrons.

They have the same atomic number (number of protons) but different mass numbers (protons + neutrons).

Hydrogen-1 (Protium): 1 proton, 0 neutrons, 1 electron
