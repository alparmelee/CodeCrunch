Fundamental Trigonometric Identities

Pythagorean Identities

sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
tan²θ + 1 = sec²θ
1 + cot²θ = csc²θ
These identities are derived from the Pythagorean theorem applied to the unit circle.
Example: Simplify sin²x + cos²x
Solution: sin²x + cos²x = 1

Reciprocal Identities

sin θ = 1/csc θ
cos θ = 1/sec θ
tan θ = 1/cot θ
These identities define the relationships between the primary and reciprocal trig functions.
Example: Simplify cos θ · sec θ
Solution: cos θ · sec θ = cos θ · (1/cos θ) = 1

Quotient Identities

tan θ = sin θ / cos θ
cot θ = cos θ / sin θ
These identities relate the tangent and cotangent functions to sine and cosine.
Example: Simplify sin θ / cos θ
Solution: sin θ / cos θ = tan θ

Co-Function Identities

sin(π/2 - θ) = cos θ
cos(π/2 - θ) = sin θ
tan(π/2 - θ) = cot θ
These identities relate functions to their co-functions at complementary angles.
Example: Find sin(π/2 - π/4)
Solution: sin(π/2 - π/4) = cos(π/4) = 1/√2 = √2/2

Step-by-Step Proofs

Proving: sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
1
Start with the Pythagorean identity: sin²θ + cos²θ
2
Recall that on the unit circle (radius = 1), a point at angle θ has coordinates (cos θ, sin θ)
3
By the Pythagorean theorem, x² + y² = r²
4
Substituting the coordinates: (cos θ)² + (sin θ)² = 1²
5
Therefore: sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
Proving: tan²θ + 1 = sec²θ
1
Start with tan²θ + 1
2
Substitute tan θ = sin θ / cos θ: (sin θ / cos θ)² + 1
3
Simplify the first term: sin²θ / cos²θ + 1
4
Find a common denominator: sin²θ / cos²θ + cos²θ / cos²θ
5
Combine the fractions: (sin²θ + cos²θ) / cos²θ
6
Use the Pythagorean identity (sin²θ + cos²θ = 1): 1 / cos²θ
7
Recall that sec θ = 1 / cos θ, so sec²θ = 1 / cos²θ
8
Therefore: tan²θ + 1 = sec²θ
