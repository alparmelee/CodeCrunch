Chapter 7: Revolutions and Enlightenment

The Age of Reason and Revolutionary Change (1650-1825 CE)

This chapter explores the intellectual movement known as the Enlightenment and its profound impact on political revolutions across the Atlantic world. Beginning with key philosophical ideas challenging traditional authority, we examine how Enlightenment principles of natural rights, reason, and liberty fueled revolutionary movements in North America, France, Haiti, and Latin America. These revolutions reshaped political structures, established new nations, and spread democratic ideals, while also revealing tensions between revolutionary rhetoric and practices like slavery and limited suffrage.
