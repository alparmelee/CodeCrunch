Basic Geometric Concepts

Point, Line, and Plane

• A point has position but no size or dimension.

• A line is a straight path that extends infinitely in two directions.

• A plane is a flat surface that extends infinitely in all directions.

Basic Postulates

• Through two distinct points, exactly one line can be drawn.

• Through three non-collinear points, exactly one plane can be drawn.

• If two points lie in a plane, then the line containing them lies in the plane.

Distance Formula

The distance between points (x₁, y₁) and (x₂, y₂) is:
d = √[(x₂ - x₁)² + (y₂ - y₁)²]
