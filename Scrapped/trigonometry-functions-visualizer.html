Understanding the Six Trigonometric Functions

Sine (sin θ)

sin θ = opposite / hypotenuse

The sine of an angle is the ratio of the length of the opposite side to the length of the hypotenuse.

Domain: All real numbers

Range: [-1, 1]

Cosine (cos θ)

cos θ = adjacent / hypotenuse

The cosine of an angle is the ratio of the length of the adjacent side to the length of the hypotenuse.

Domain: All real numbers

Range: [-1, 1]

Tangent (tan θ)

tan θ = opposite / adjacent = sin θ / cos θ

The tangent of an angle is the ratio of the sine to the cosine of that angle.

Domain: All real numbers except θ = (π/2) + nπ, where n is an integer

Range: All real numbers

Cosecant (csc θ)

csc θ = hypotenuse / opposite = 1 / sin θ

The cosecant of an angle is the reciprocal of the sine of that angle.

Domain: All real numbers except θ = nπ, where n is an integer

Range: (-∞, -1] ∪ [1, ∞)

Secant (sec θ)

sec θ = hypotenuse / adjacent = 1 / cos θ

The secant of an angle is the reciprocal of the cosine of that angle.

Domain: All real numbers except θ = (π/2) + nπ, where n is an integer

Range: (-∞, -1] ∪ [1, ∞)

Cotangent (cot θ)

cot θ = adjacent / opposite = cos θ / sin θ = 1 / tan θ

The cotangent of an angle is the reciprocal of the tangent of that angle.

Domain: All real numbers except θ = nπ, where n is an integer

Range: All real numbers

Relationships Between Trigonometric Functions

Key Relationships:

  • sin² θ + cos² θ = 1 (Pythagorean identity)
  • tan θ = sin θ / cos θ
  • cot θ = cos θ / sin θ
  • csc θ = 1 / sin θ
  • sec θ = 1 / cos θ
  • cot θ = 1 / tan θ
