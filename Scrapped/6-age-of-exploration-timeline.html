Chapter 6: Age of Exploration and Global Exchange

European Expansion and the Birth of Global Networks (1400-1700 CE)

This chapter examines the unprecedented era of European maritime exploration that connected distant continents and cultures. Beginning with Portuguese and Spanish voyages along the African coast and across the Atlantic, we trace how these journeys established global trade networks, colonial empires, and the Columbian Exchange of plants, animals, diseases, and ideas. These encounters transformed societies worldwide, created new economic systems, and led to both cultural exchange and devastating exploitation through slavery and conquest.
