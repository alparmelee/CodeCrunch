Chapter 3: World Religions and Belief Systems

The Origins and Spread of Major World Religions (1500 BCE-1500 CE)

This chapter explores how five major world religions—Hinduism, Buddhism, Judaism, Christianity, and Islam—emerged and spread across regions, profoundly shaping societies, legal systems, cultural practices, and human history. Through sacred texts, key figures, and foundational beliefs, these traditions established frameworks for understanding humanity's place in the cosmos and guidelines for ethical living that continue to influence billions of people today.
