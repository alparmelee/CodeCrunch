Producers offer more of a good as its price increases and less as its price falls.

üî¢ Quantity Supplied

üìà Market Supply Curve

üîÑ Elasticity of Supply

üîÑ Variable

Production Factors

üë∑ Marginal Product of Labor

The change in output from hiring one additional unit of labor.

üìà Increasing Marginal Returns

A level of production in which the marginal product of labor increases as the number of workers increases.

üìâ Diminishing Marginal Returns

A level of production in which the marginal product of labor decreases as the number of workers increases.

üß± Fixed Cost

üìä Variable Cost

A cost that rises or falls depending on the quantity produced.

üí∞ Total Cost

The sum of fixed costs plus variable costs.

‚ûï Marginal Cost

üíµ Marginal Revenue

üìä Average Cost

The total cost divided by the quantity produced.

üè≠ Operating Cost

The cost of operating a facility, such as a store or factory.

üí∞ Excise Tax
