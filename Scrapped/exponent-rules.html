Exponent Rules

Multiplication with Same Base
am × an = am+n

When multiplying expressions with the same base, add the exponents.

Example: 23 × 24 = 23+4 = 27 = 128

Division with Same Base
am ÷ an = am-n

When dividing expressions with the same base, subtract the exponents.

Example: 27 ÷ 23 = 27-3 = 24 = 16

Power of a Power
(am)n = am×n

When raising a power to another power, multiply the exponents.

Example: (23)2 = 23×2 = 26 = 64

Power of a Product
(a × b)n = an × bn

When raising a product to a power, distribute the exponent to each factor.

Example: (2 × 3)2 = 22 × 32 = 4 × 9 = 36

Power of a Quotient
(a ÷ b)n = an ÷ bn

When raising a quotient to a power, distribute the exponent to both numerator and denominator.

Example: (4 ÷ 2)3 = 43 ÷ 23 = 64 ÷ 8 = 8

Negative Exponents
a-n = 1 ÷ an = 1/an

A negative exponent indicates the reciprocal of the base raised to the positive exponent.

Example: 2-3 = 1 ÷ 23 = 1/8 = 0.125

Zero Exponent
a0 = 1 (where a ≠ 0)

Any non-zero number raised to the power of zero equals 1.

Example: 70 = 1

Fractional Exponents
am/n = n√am = (n√a)m

A fractional exponent indicates a root with the denominator as the root index and the numerator as the power.

Example: 82/3 = (∛8)2 = 22 = 4

Scientific Notation

What is Scientific Notation?

Scientific notation expresses numbers as a product of a coefficient between 1 and 10 and a power of 10:

a × 10n

where 1 ≤ a < 10 and n is an integer.

Converting to Scientific Notation

To convert a number to scientific notation:

  1. Move the decimal point so that there is exactly one non-zero digit to the left of the decimal point
  2. Count the number of places the decimal point moved
  3. If you moved the decimal point to the left, the exponent is positive
  4. If you moved the decimal point to the right, the exponent is negative

Example: 4,570,000 = 4.57 × 106

Example: 0.00034 = 3.4 × 10-4

Operations with Scientific Notation

When performing operations with numbers in scientific notation, follow these rules:

Multiplication: (a × 10m) × (b × 10n) = (a × b) × 10m+n

Example: (3 × 104) × (2 × 10-2) = 6 × 102 = 600

Division: (a × 10m) ÷ (b × 10n) = (a ÷ b) × 10m-n

Example: (8 × 105) ÷ (4 × 102) = 2 × 103 = 2,000
