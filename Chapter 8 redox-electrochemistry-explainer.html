1. Oxidation-Reduction (Redox) Reactions

Redox reactions involve the transfer of electrons between chemical species. They consist of two half-reactions:

  • Oxidation: Loss of electrons (LEO - Loss of Electrons is Oxidation)
  • Reduction: Gain of electrons (GER - Gain of Electrons is Reduction)

Oxidation Number: A concept used to track electron transfers. In redox reactions:

  • Increase in oxidation number = Oxidation
  • Decrease in oxidation number = Reduction

  • Copper is reduced (gains 2 electrons): Cu²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Cu

Common Oxidizing and Reducing Agents

MnO₄⁻, Cr₂O₇²⁻, H₂O₂ Fe²⁺, I⁻, S²⁻, C

Example: Consider the reaction between magnesium and oxygen:

2Mg(s) + O₂(g) → 2MgO(s)

  • Mg is oxidized from 0 to +2
  • O is reduced from 0 to -2
  • Mg is the reducing agent
  • O₂ is the oxidizing agent

2. Electrochemical Cells

Electrochemical cells convert chemical energy into electrical energy (or vice versa) through redox reactions.

Galvanic/Voltaic Cells (Spontaneous)

These cells produce electricity from spontaneous redox reactions.

Zinc-Copper Galvanic Cell

Cell Components:

  • Anode: Zinc electrode (oxidation occurs) - negative terminal
  • Cathode: Copper electrode (reduction occurs) - positive terminal
  • Salt Bridge: Allows ion movement to maintain electrical neutrality
  • External Circuit: Path for electrons to flow from anode to cathode

Cell Notation: Zn(s) | Zn²⁺(aq) || Cu²⁺(aq) | Cu(s)

Cell Potential (Voltage)

The cell potential (E°cell) is the measure of the driving force of the redox reaction:

E°cell = E°cathode - E°anode

For our Zn-Cu cell: E°cell = +0.34V - (-0.76V) = +1.10V

Electrolytic Cells (Non-spontaneous)

These cells use electrical energy to drive non-spontaneous redox reactions.

Common applications include electroplating and the production of aluminum and chlorine.

3. Batteries

Batteries are portable electrochemical cells designed to provide electrical energy.

Types of Batteries

Battery Type Chemistry Applications
Alkaline Zn/MnO₂ Household devices
Lithium-ion LiCoO₂/Graphite Electronics, EVs
Lead-acid Pb/PbO₂ Car batteries
Nickel-Cadmium Ni(OH)₂/Cd Power tools

Lithium-ion Battery

Half-reactions:

  • Anode: LiC₆ → C₆ + Li⁺ + e⁻
  • Cathode: Li₁₋ₓCoO₂ + xLi⁺ + xe⁻ → LiCoO₂

Advantages: High energy density, low self-discharge, no memory effect

Summary

  • Redox Reactions: Involve electron transfer with oxidation (loss of electrons) and reduction (gain of electrons)
  • Galvanic Cells: Spontaneous redox reactions produce electricity
  • Electrolytic Cells: Use electricity to drive non-spontaneous redox reactions
  • Batteries: Portable electrochemical cells with various chemistries for different applications
