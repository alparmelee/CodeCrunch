9. Proof by Contradiction Example

Statement: Given integers a and b, if ab is an even number, then a or b is even.

Proof (by Contradiction):

Assume the statement is false. This means we assume the hypothesis (ab is even) is true AND the conclusion (a or b is even) is false.

The negation of "a or b is even" is "a is NOT even AND b is NOT even", which means "a is odd AND b is odd".

So, our assumption for the contradiction is: ab is even, and both a and b are odd integers.

By the definition of odd integers, we can write a = 2k + 1 and b = 2j + 1 for some integers k and j.

Now, let's multiply a and b:
ab = (2k + 1)(2j + 1)

Expand the product:
ab = 4kj + 2k + 2j + 1

Factor out a 2 from the first three terms:
ab = 2(2kj + k + j) + 1

Let m = 2kj + k + j. Since k and j are integers, m must also be an integer (as integers are closed under multiplication and addition).

So, we can write ab = 2m + 1. By definition, this means that ab is an odd integer.

This contradicts our initial assumption that ab was even.

Since our assumption (that ab is even AND both a and b are odd) leads to a contradiction, the assumption must be false. Therefore, the original statement must be true.

Conclusion: If ab is even, then at least one of a or b must be even.
