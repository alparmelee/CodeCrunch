GCD and Modular Inverse Questions

Fall 2022 Quiz #1

(10 pts) Determine 23^-1 mod 108.

Answer: 47

We use the Extended Euclidean Algorithm to find the inverse. 108 = 4 * 23 + 16 23 = 1 * 16 + 7 16 = 2 * 7 + 2 7 = 3 * 2 + 1 Working backwards: 1 = 7 - 3 * 2 1 = 7 - 3(16 - 2 * 7) = 7 - 3 * 16 + 6 * 7 = 7 * 7 - 3 * 16 1 = 7(23 - 1 * 16) - 3 * 16 = 7 * 23 - 7 * 16 - 3 * 16 = 7 * 23 - 10 * 16 1 = 7 * 23 - 10(108 - 4 * 23) = 7 * 23 - 10 * 108 + 40 * 23 = 47 * 23 - 10 * 108 Therefore, 47 * 23 is equivalent to 1 mod 108, so the inverse is 47.
Fall 2021 Quiz #1

(15 pts) Using the Extended Euclidean Algorithm determine 108^-1 mod 239. Please answer with an integer in between 0 and 238, inclusive. Note: most of the credit will be for the steps of the algorithm and not the final answer.

Answer: 135

We use the Extended Euclidean Algorithm to find the inverse. 239 = 2 * 108 + 23 108 = 4 * 23 + 16 23 = 1 * 16 + 7 16 = 2 * 7 + 2 7 = 3 * 2 + 1 Working backwards: 1 = 7 - 3 * 2 1 = 7 - 3(16 - 2 * 7) = 7 - 3 * 16 + 6 * 7 = 7 * 7 - 3 * 16 1 = 7(23 - 1 * 16) - 3 * 16 = 7 * 23 - 7 * 16 - 3 * 16 = 7 * 23 - 10 * 16 1 = 7 * 23 - 10(108 - 4 * 23) = 7 * 23 - 10 * 108 + 40 * 23 = 47 * 23 - 10 * 108 1 = 47(239 - 2 * 108) - 10 * 108 = 47 * 239 - 94 * 108 - 10 * 108 = 47 * 239 - 104 * 108 Therefore, -104 * 108 is equivalent to 1 mod 239. The inverse is -104 + 239 = 135.
Fall 2020 Quiz #1

(6 pts) Determine the greatest common divisor of 336 and 142 using the Euclidean Algorithm.

Answer: 2

336 = 2 * 142 + 52 142 = 2 * 52 + 38 52 = 1 * 38 + 14 38 = 2 * 14 + 10 14 = 1 * 10 + 4 10 = 2 * 4 + 2 4 = 2 * 2 + 0 The last non-zero remainder is the GCD.
