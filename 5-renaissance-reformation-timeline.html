Chapter 5: Renaissance and Reformation

Rebirth of Classical Knowledge and Religious Reform in Europe (1300-1650 CE)

This chapter explores the intellectual, artistic, and religious transformations that reshaped Europe following the Late Middle Ages. Beginning with the Italian Renaissance's revival of classical learning and artistic innovation, we trace how humanist thinking, scientific inquiry, and religious reform movements fundamentally altered European society. The period witnessed unprecedented achievements in art, literature, and science, alongside religious upheaval that permanently fractured Western Christianity.
