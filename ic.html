Fall 2020 Quiz #1

(10 pts) Consider a set of letters with 1 A, 2 Bs, 3 Cs, 4 Ds, ..., and 26 Zs. What is the index of coincidence of this set of letters? Express your answer as a fraction in lowest terms.

Answer: 13/287

The number of letters is the sum of the first 26 integers: $N = \sum_{i=1}^{26} i = \frac{26(27)}{2} = 13 \cdot 27 = 351$. The numerator of the IC formula is $\sum_{i=1}^{26} n_i(n_i-1)$, where $n_i$ is the count of each letter. In this case, $n_i = i$. Numerator = $\sum_{i=1}^{26} i(i-1) = \sum_{i=1}^{26} (i^2 - i)$ Using the sum of squares and sum of integers formulas: $\sum_{i=1}^{26} i^2 = \frac{26(27)(2 \cdot 26 + 1)}{6} = \frac{26 \cdot 27 \cdot 53}{6} = 13 \cdot 9 \cdot 53 = 117 \cdot 53 = 6201$ $\sum_{i=1}^{26} i = \frac{26(27)}{2} = 351$ Numerator = $6201 - 351 = 5850$ The denominator of the IC formula is $N(N-1) = 351(350)$. IC = $\frac{5850}{351 \cdot 350} = \frac{585}{351 \cdot 35} = \frac{117}{351 \cdot 7} = \frac{1}{3 \cdot 7} = \frac{1}{21}$. Let's check the hint provided in the original document. Sum $1 \times 2 + 2 \times 3 + \dots + (n-1) \times n = \frac{(n-1)n(n+1)}{3}$ Here, we have a sum from 1 to 25: $\sum_{i=1}^{25} i(i+1)$. This is not quite the same. The sum is $\sum_{i=1}^{26} n_i(n_i-1) = \sum_{i=1}^{26} i(i-1)$. This matches the sum of $1 \times 0 + 2 \times 1 + 3 \times 2 + \dots + 26 \times 25$. Using the hint with $n=26$: $\frac{(26-1)(26)(26+1)}{3} = \frac{25 \cdot 26 \cdot 27}{3} = 25 \cdot 26 \cdot 9 = 5850$. This confirms our numerator calculation. IC = $\frac{5850}{351 \cdot 350} = \frac{585}{351 \cdot 35} = \frac{117}{351 \cdot 7}$. $117 = 9 \cdot 13$ $351 = 9 \cdot 39 = 9 \cdot 3 \cdot 13 = 27 \cdot 13$ IC = $\frac{9 \cdot 13}{27 \cdot 13 \cdot 7} = \frac{9}{27 \cdot 7} = \frac{1}{3 \cdot 7} = \frac{1}{21}$.
